# THE MEMBRANE MANIFESTO

## A Constitutional Grammar for the Age of Artificial Intelligence

---

### I. THE PROBLEM

A board of directors meets to approve a strategic decision. The resolution was drafted by an AI system. The risk language was generated by it. The comparative analysis was produced by it. The directors read, ask questions, deliberate, and sign. The minutes record what was decided. They do not record how the decision formed.

Two years later, something goes wrong, and a derivative plaintiff asks the obvious question: **who actually decided this?**

The AI did not vote. It also did not need to. Its work shaped what the humans saw when they voted. The record cannot show where independent human judgment was exercised at the threshold of decision.

This is not a hypothetical. Over 1,300 court filings already document the pattern in the lawyer-fabrication cases — *Mata v. Avianca*, *Johnson v. Dunn*, *Buchanan v. Vuori*. Generative AI produced substantive work. A fiduciary presented it as their own. The record could not show where the machine stopped and the human began.

Lawyers paid in sanctions. Directors will pay in derivative settlements, in D&O exclusions, and in personal exposure where coverage was bought without contemplating the risk.

Existing frameworks — NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, the EU AI Act, SEC guidance — all describe what organizations should do *around* their AI systems. None of them produce a structurally verifiable record of where, in any specific decision, the substantive judgment formed.

Regulation governs what organizations report. It does not govern what happens at the moment a human director either exercises judgment or ratifies output.

**This is the language gap.** And it will not be closed by more regulation. It will be closed by a grammar.


217 bytes. SHA-256 pinned. Immutable.

**Line 1** is the constitution: Human holds the question. AI holds the patterns. The vertical bar — the Membrane — is where the decision forms, with the AI's contribution visible and recorded but not collapsing into the human's signature.

**Lines 2–7** are the cycle: Start → Growth → Quality → Power → Value. Five phases. Every genuine decision moves through them, whether you name them or not.

**Line 8** is the completion rule: No cycle closes without opening a new question. A decision memo that ends with "Therefore, we will do X" is incomplete. It must add: "The question this decision opens is..."

**Line 9** is the security layer: Five corruption codes that detect specific structural failures — closing too early, letting AI drive, claiming authority you don't have, performing deliberation without substance, ending without a new question.

The same five codes. The same structural function. Whether you govern a sole proprietorship, a small business, a foundation, a DAO, or a civilization.

### III. THE MEMBRANE

Imagine a thin line drawn between two domains — human judgment and machine computation.

The line is not a wall. It is a membrane: porous in one direction, structural in the other. Information flows across it. Authorization does not.

The human side is the side where the question was opened. The machine side is the side where the patterns are recombined. The decision is made **at the line** — by the human, with the machine's contribution visible and recorded, but not collapsing into the human's signature.

This is not a metaphor. It is an architecture:

- **DEFINITE claims** are machine-checkable in real time: Was the decision recorded with the Codex hash? Did the phase order hold? Is the attestation signed?
- **HEURISTIC claims** are machine-detected and human-confirmed: The watcher flags a pattern that looks like AI-generated direction. A human reviews and confirms or dismisses.
- **ATTESTATION_REQUIRED claims** are human-governed and structurally protected from any machine judgment: Did the question arrive from genuine not-knowing, or was it manufactured? Only a human can attest. No AI is asked to verify.

The Membrane is not a policy. It is not a code of conduct. It is a **fiduciary duty** — owed by each decision-maker to the organization, verifiable in part by a deterministic checker that needs no AI to run, examinable by a court in 2050 against artifacts sealed in 2026.

We call it the **Duty of Membrane Integrity.** It belongs in the operating agreement. It belongs in the bylaws. It belongs wherever humans make decisions assisted by machines.
