# Your First Decision Record

You're about to produce your first S-BDR. It takes 10 minutes. Use a real business decision — the next one you face today, however small. Pricing a project. Choosing a tool. Responding to a client email that requires thought.

---

## Step 1: Hold the Question (2 minutes)

Before you open an AI agent, before you Google, before you ask anyone — sit with the question.

Not "what should I do?" That's not a question. That's outsourcing.

A genuine question sounds like:
- "What is the right price for the value I actually delivered — not the market average, not what they'll pay, but what's fair?"
- "What does this business need to become — not what do I need it to stay?"
- "What am I avoiding by staying busy?"

Write it down. This is your **S-phase.**

---

## Step 2: Name the Pattern (2 minutes)

What's the irreducible core of this situation? If you had to name it in one sentence — something that, if you removed it, the whole situation would fall apart?

This is your **α** (alpha). Examples:
- "Value-based pricing, not cost-plus"
- "Aliveness is the binding constraint on sustainability"
- "The business is no longer an extension of me"

Name it yourself. Don't ask an AI to name it. If you can't name it yet, you're not done with Step 1.

---

## Step 3: Intersect Your Perception with the AI's Analysis (3 minutes)

Now you can open your AI agent. Ask it to illuminate — not to recommend:
- "What are comparable rates for this kind of work?"
- "What patterns do you see in the market for this?"
- "What constraints am I not seeing?"

The AI provides **Ω** (the universal context — data, patterns, constraints).

You bring **φ** (your direct perception — what you actually experience, not theory).

Where they meet is **Z** — the specific moment something locked. Write it down. Be specific. Not "this feels right." More like: "The AI's data shows clients in my segment pay 20-35% more for rush work. My direct experience is that this client values speed over price — they've never questioned an invoice. The intersection: a rush premium here isn't a penalty, it's reflecting the value they already perceive."

---

## Step 4: Find the Gradient (2 minutes)

Where does the energy naturally want to go? Not "what should I do?" — what's already pulling?

- Energy wasted: defending low rates, justifying your value, negotiating with yourself
- Value discovered: one honest conversation about price, one clear proposal, one signed contract

The gradient (**∇**) is the path of least resistance. It's usually the thing you're avoiding because it requires courage, not because it requires effort.

Write it down: "∇ → Have the pricing conversation directly, don't bury it in a spreadsheet."

---

## Step 5: Crystallize and Open (1 minute)

What's the actual artifact? The proposal. The email. The contract. The decision recorded here.

What question does this decision open? One you couldn't have asked before this cycle. Must end with **?**

Write it: "What other relationships am I underpricing — not just financially, but in attention, energy, and care?"

---

## Step 6: Sign the Membrane Attestation (30 seconds)

```
I attest that independent human judgment was exercised at the threshold
of this decision. AI contributions are listed below and are bounded.
The α was named from direct recognition, not from AI suggestion.
The decision was made at the Membrane — with the AI's contribution
visible and recorded, but not collapsing into my signature.
```

Sign it. Date it. Record the AI tools you used and exactly what they contributed.
