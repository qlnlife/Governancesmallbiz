## Part III — What a Day Looks Like

### Scenario: Small Consultancy (5 people, one owner)

**9:00 AM** — Owner faces a pricing decision. She's been undercharging a client for 6 months. She asks Claude to analyze comparable rates and draft a proposal. Claude produces excellent work.

**Before 5QLN:** She reads Claude's output, tweaks a few numbers, sends it. The record shows "Owner decided." In reality, Claude did 90% of the substantive work. If the client later disputes the rate increase and asks "who actually set these numbers?" — there's no verifiable answer.

**With 5QLN (small business adapter):**
1. **S-phase** — She holds the question: "What is the fair price for this work, given the value delivered and the market?" She writes this in the decision record. (30 seconds)
2. **G-phase** — The pattern surfaces: "α: Value-based pricing, not cost-plus or market-average." She names it. (30 seconds)
3. **Q-phase** — She tests the pattern. Does value-based pricing hold against the client's budget constraints? The actual relationship? The market for this specific service? Claude provides comparables — tagged as AI input. (5 minutes)
4. **P-phase** — The gradient becomes clear: the energy wasted on justifying low rates > the energy of one honest conversation about value. ∇ → "Have the conversation, don't send the spreadsheet." (2 minutes)
5. **V-phase** — She crystallizes: the proposal is the B'' artifact. The decision record is completed. ∞0': "What other relationships am I underpricing — not just financially, but in attention, energy, and care?" (2 minutes)

**Total overhead: ~10 minutes.** Artifact produced: a decision record that shows where AI contributed and where human judgment formed. If the client disputes, the record is verifiable. If the owner wants to review in 6 months, the trace is intact.

### Scenario: Product Company (12 people, two co-founders)

The co-founders disagree about a strategic hire. One wants a CTO-level engineer. The other wants to invest in marketing.

**Without 5QLN:** They debate. Whoever argues better wins. The loser resents it. The decision gets recorded as "After discussion, the Board resolved to..." with no trace of how the resolution formed.

**With 5QLN:** They run the cycle together.
- **S:** "What is the right next hire for where we actually are, not where we wish we were?"

They find the intersection: the real constraint is neither engineering nor marketing — it's customer discovery.
- **P:** ∇ → "Hire a head of product who does customer discovery. The CTO hire and marketing spend both follow from better customer understanding."
- **V:** B'' is the job description for Head of Product. ∞0': "What assumption about our customers are we most afraid to test?"

The decision record captures the AI contribution (market data, projections), the human intersection (each founder's direct perception meeting the data), and the gradient — why the third option emerged from the process rather than from either founder's initial position.

## Part V — Open Questions

1. **Sole proprietorship:** Can one person genuinely hold the Membrane? If the same person generates the AI input AND attests to the Membrane integrity, is the attestation structurally meaningful or is it theater?

   **Answer (from stress test, June 2026):** Yes — with a structural condition. The sole proprietor must be willing to produce the record honestly, even when it reveals failure. The S-BDR format forces this: you can't write "I was angry and wanted AI to validate me" and then sign the attestation. The grammar catches you. **But the Membrane has no enforcement against a lie.** If the sole proprietor is willing to falsify the record, there is no second human to challenge it.

   **Finding:** The minimum viable governance unit is **one human who means it** — but the minimum *enforceable* governance unit is **two.** The Membrane has two functions: diagnostic (catches structural failure — works alone) and enforcement (allows challenge — requires at least a dyad). A sole proprietor gets diagnostic. A partnership gets both.

   Full stress test: see `governance/membrane-stress-test-sole-proprietorship.md`

2. **Multi-member vs. single-member LLC:** The Duty of Membrane Integrity has teeth when there are multiple members who can enforce it against each other. In a single-member LLC, who enforces? The answer may be: the owner's future self, through the audit trail. Is that enough?

3. **Cost-benefit for very small businesses:** If the cycle adds 10 minutes to a decision, and the business makes 5 material decisions per week, that's ~40 hours/year — one work week. Is the verifiability worth a week of time? For a consultancy facing liability, yes. For a coffee shop, probably not. Where's the threshold?

4. **The AI Operating Manual — small business edition:** The AI OS Edition Bylaws currently say "I AM DERIVATIVE. My authority is specified, bounded, and subordinate."
